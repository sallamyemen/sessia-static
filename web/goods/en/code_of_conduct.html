Appendix to the Partnership Agreement

Company Partner Code of Conduct

The Company Partner Code of Conduct (Code) is a set of the most important rules and ethical standards that define the functions, responsibilities and rights of Partners in relation to the Company, each other, and the Company’s clients.
By registering with the Company, each Partner undertakes to comply with this Code, which is an integral part of the Partnership Agreement.
The Company reserves the right to create and implement other rules and regulations, the introduction of which is communicated to Partners in official Company sources.
1. General Provisions
1.1.

The Partner carries out independent activities to promote the Company and its Products. Their image, professionalism, speech, appearance, and internal culture must meet the Company's high requirements.

1.2.

The Partner undertakes to care for the Company's reputation, adhering to high ethical principles consistent with the spirit of cooperation among Partners.

1.3.

The Partner knows the Products well, uses them, and can convincingly recommend them to others by personal example.

1.4.

The Partner presents the Products in strict accordance with the description in the official documents of the Company. The Partner must not interpret the description of the Company’s Products and services in their own way; false statements about the effectiveness of products and possible income are unacceptable.

1.5.

The Partner refrains from putting pressure on potential Clients and Partners, respecting their right to make independent decisions about the value of the information provided and about cooperation with the Company.

1.6.

Each Partner must maintain correct relations with all other Partners of the Company and treat them carefully and kindly, regardless of their status and whether they are members of its structure or not.

1.7.

The Partner is prohibited from promoting methods of obtaining various types of third-party income that differ from the types of incentives listed in official Company documents.

1.8.

To the extent possible, the Partner shall prevent ethical violations by promptly reporting any illegal activities carried out by other Partners to Company employees.

1.9.

The Partner is obliged to conduct business so that their activities are correct from the point of view of the law and do not damage the reputation of the Company and its Partners.

1.10.

he Partner knows the Code, complies with it always and everywhere, and is aware that violation of the paragraphs of this Code and the Partnership Agreement may lead to termination of registration with the Company.

2. Registration with the Company
2.1.

A Company Partner can be:

  • any capable person over 18 years of age;
  • any legal entity created in the manner prescribed by the legislation in force at the place of its registration;
  • in exceptional cases - a person who has reached the age of 14, unless this is prohibited by the legislation in force in the Partner’s location. Such a decision is made individually based on the application of such a person and their potential Guarantor.
2.2.

Registration of a Partner with the Company is allowed only by a mobile phone number whose SIM card is issued in their name.

2.3.

By confirming registration with the Company, the Partner undertakes:

  • indicate reliable information about themself, including personal data necessary for withdrawing funds, accruing rewards and other financial transactions carried out within the framework of cooperation with the Company;
  • to not re-register or create new Personal Accounts. Double registration with the Company is strictly prohibited;
  • to not post photographs of other people or other images in which their face is not present as their avatar in their Personal Account. The optimal avatar is a photograph of the Partner with a calm, friendly expression and open eyes, taken from the front or half-turned against a neutral background without other people or objects in the frame.
2.4.

The company reserves the right to refuse registration without giving reasons.

3. Relations between the Partner and the Company
3.1.

The Company provides all Partners, free of charge and on a reimbursable basis, with Products, information, materials, and opportunities that they can use for personal needs and to attract new Partners and Clients to cooperate with the Company, namely:

  • official Company documents, including compensation plans and privilege programs;
  • company products for purchases and as gifts;
  • access to websites, mobile applications and other IT products that the Company considers necessary for informing Partners and Clients, making purchases, as well as for Partners to carry out activities to promote the Company and its Products;
  • marketing and advertising materials for Products and the Company (booklets, catalogs, presentations, leaflets, etc.);
  • training and educational materials on Products and the Company;
  • providing information about events (trips, conferences, webinars, etc.) conducted by the Company for Partners or with their participation, and about the conditions for participation in these events through the Application, social networks, e-mail newsletters, and other sources of information dissemination.
3.2.

The Partner undertakes to:

  • carry out activities aimed at promoting Products and the Company and attracting new Partners for cooperation with the Company;
  • carry out personal purchasing activities necessary to maintain previously completed privileges under the Company Privilege Program. In particular, to participate in the Company’s trips, the Partner must make monthly purchases from the Company for 6 calendar months before the start of the trip;
  • strictly comply with corporate style requirements and branding rules. These primarily include logos, names, and slogans of the Company and its brands, images, labels, product names and slogans, and corporate colors. Distortion and any other changes to logos, packaging, and labels are prohibited;
  • not dump, i.e., not to sell the Company’s products at prices lower than those indicated on the Company’s website for a specific Partner country;
  • not to demand a refund for a pre-order of the Company’s Products in the event of a delay in its production for reasons beyond the Company’s control;
  • use and distribute only those relevant marketing and advertising materials provided or approved by the Company;
  • not create their own advertising and marketing materials (printed, electronic, etc.) to promote the Company’s products, services, and brand without the consent of the Company;
  • not conduct activities that contradict the Partnership Agreement and this Code;
  • not cause any material damage to the Company;
  • not discredit the Company, its Products, and employees, and not disseminate negative information and false information regarding the Company and its employees and other Partners during and after completion of cooperation with the Company. “Negative information” means any negative statement containing false information about the Company and its employees, other Partners, made in the public domain, including public and online announcements, statements calling into question the reputation of the Company and degrading the dignity of its employees and other Partners;
  • not make inflated promises to potential Partners and Clients, attracting them to purchases and cooperation with the Company;
  • not commit fraud and other various types of unscrupulous actions in order to receive increased points, bonuses, and other benefits under the Company’s Privilege Programs, including “fake” registrations for figureheads, re-registration under new phone numbers, placing personal orders for Partners with a greater discount and etc.;
  • place orders at the nearest Company store, which the Company application selects as the main one for the Partner. Otherwise, the Company reserves the right to cancel the purchase;
  • not use the Company’s offices and stores for purposes unrelated to promoting the Company, its brands, and its Products.
3.3.

To stimulate Partner loyalty and activity, the Company has developed Company Privilege Programs. According to their terms, the Partner, by making purchases personally and attracting new Clients and Partners, gets the opportunity to accumulate points, improve statuses and receive various privileges, including:

  • holding events at the expense of the Company. The Company announces the amount for each type of event. The Company makes payment only after agreeing on the program, list of participants, and location of the event;
  • special training programs, upon completion of which participants receive appropriate certificates from the Company;
  • travel at the expense of the Company. The company organizes trips and pays for flights, accommodation, and a previously agreed program. All unscheduled events, the participation of children and other close relatives in the trip (except for family contracts, if the required amount of points has been accumulated) are to be paid by the Partner independently;
  • car at the expense of the Company. The company independently selects the make and model of the car, approves it with the Partner, and purchases it.
3.4.

The Company reserves the right to cancel any privileges if, within the last 6 months, the Partner:

  • did not make purchases from the Company;
  • did not log into the Company application;
  • did not take part in online or offline company events;
  • did not publish a single post in the Application News Feed;
  • violated the requirements listed in clause 3.2.
3.5.

The Partner has the right to refuse the privileges offered to him by notifying the Company at least one month before the start of their provision. The issue of accumulated points is resolved by agreement of the parties.

3.6.

To participate in forums, conferences, and other offline events (except for trips at the Company's expense), the Partner pays for travel (air, train tickets, and other types) and accommodation at their own expense unless otherwise agreed by the Company.

3.7.

The Company has the right to change the date of offline events for Partners or with their participation, having previously notified them of such changes at least 14 days before such events start. In case of postponement of an event, the Company does not accept claims from the Partner for previously paid travel under clause 3.6.

4. Relationships between Partners
4.1.

The Company provides equal open opportunities to all Partners and monitors that the principles of equality and mutual respect are observed in relationships between Partners.

4.2.

The Partner treats all Partners with care and kindness, regardless of their status and whether they are members of their group or not.

4.3.

The Partner must not take actions aimed at destroying the structures of other Partners and interfere with their activities, including:

  • inviting Partners or Clients from other structures to move to your structure or force them to make this transition;
  • inducing or forcing Partners or Clients to terminate cooperation with the Company;
  • attracting a person invited by another Partner into your structure;
  • making commercial and business offers to Partners and Clients from other structures related to the Company's or other companies' activities.
5. Re-signing
5.1.

ПRe-signing is the re-registration of a Partner, their Guarantor, and members of their group if they have a valid registration number, as well as submission of a new registration form for registration if there is a valid registration number or in violation of clause 10.6 of the Partnership Agreement.

Re-signing is also the submission of a registration form by the Partner’s spouse, their other relatives, their Guarantor, and members of their group to another group without the Company's prior written consent.

5.2.

The partner strictly adheres to the rule of maintaining the stability of their groups - they do not commit internal re-signing.

6. Rules of conduct in the Company's offices and stores
6.1.

The Company's offices and stores are an area with equal opportunities for all Partners. Compliance with these Rules of Conduct is mandatory for all Partners.

6.2.

On the territory of the Company’s office and store, it is prohibited to:

6.2.1.

Sell and advertise the goods and services of other companies, conduct consultations, presentations, trainings, and other events dedicated to the goods, services and activities of other companies.

6.2.2.

Conduct medical consultations, diagnostics and other therapeutic and health procedures. Any medical activity carried out in public places requires state licensing. Such actions are contrary to the principles and rules of the Company.

6.2.3.

Organize watch duties to recruit potential clients or partners entering the Company’s offices and stores. Long, unreasonable stay of Partners on the trading floor is considered as being watch duty. It is recommended to hold meetings with clients, training and master classes in specially designated areas within offices and stores.

6.2.4.

Behave incorrectly towards employees of the Company’s offices, stores, and other Partners (allow insults, rudeness, intemperance, and obscene language).

6.2.5.

Require employees of the Company's offices and stores to perform actions that are not part of their job duties.

6.2.6.

Disturb public order (talk loudly, be drunk on the territory of the Company's offices and stores, use physical force to sort things out with visitors or employees of the Company's offices and stores, behave immorally, etc.).

6.2.7.

Eating food, consuming alcoholic beverages, narcotics, and toxic substances on the territory of the Company’s offices and stores.

6.2.8.

Intervene in the process of servicing customers, clients, and Partners by employees of the Company’s offices and stores.

6.2.9.

Violate the work schedule established in the Company’s offices and stores.

6.2.10.

Conduct paid events (schools, trainings, master classes, etc.) in the Company’s offices and stores.

7. Rules of conduct for a Partner on social networks and the Company’s application feed
7.1.

The Company grants all Partners the right to freely promote Products and the Company in the public space, introducing themselves as a Partner of the Company, using branding attributes of the Company and its brands (logo, name, etc.) and posting posts and other materials as a Partner of the Company on social networks, the feed of the Company’s mobile applications, Telegram channels and other resources.

7.2.

Acting as a Partner of the Company in a public space, the Partner must not post materials with the following content:

  • politics, religion, and various kinds of convictions;
  • pets (unless related to the Products and the Company);
  • hobbies if they are in no way related to the Company and its Products;
  • events and stories unrelated to our products or company;
  • the products of other companies;
  • beneficial plants and active ingredients that are not included in our products;
  • personal tragedies and dramas other than those that have a happy ending thanks to our products or company;
  • negative thoughts, phenomena, or events;
  • meaningless or provocative - do not write posts for the sake of simply writing.
7.3.

What the Partner has the right to write about:

  • about your group and its life: how your Partners live, how they achieve success and new ranks, what problems and in what ways you solve them together;
  • about yourself, about what motivates you, what thoughts and actions help you get set for success and achieve high results;
  • about how you use your bonuses and privileges received from the Company;
  • about your family and your life, about how, thanks to the Company, you improve the health of your loved ones, have the opportunity to give your children a prestigious education, travel and spend more time with them;
  • about your work as a team, how you attract newcomers, conduct meetings and events, what techniques you use, and how they help you;
  • about the Products, their advantages, and useful properties: how you use them, how they help you and have helped other specific people, about their active ingredients, about new products and proven hits;
  • about the Company and its President: how new products are created to help you maintain health and lead an active lifestyle, about the recognition and gifts that you and your partners receive from it;
  • about traveling with the Company and our events;
  • about the Company's promotions and contests that you and your team can participate in;
  • about events that take place in your team.
8. Violation of the Code
8.1.

A Partner who becomes aware of a violation of one or more rules of the Code by another Partner is obliged to inform the violator about this, discuss their action with them, and explain the purpose of the Rule. Most violations result from misunderstanding, and most often, calm discussion and clarification are sufficient to resolve the problem. If the violator understands the rule and agrees to act in accordance with it, there is no need to report the violation to the Company.

8.2.

If the violator refuses to act in accordance with the rule of the Code, the Partner may submit a statement to the Company through the technical support function in the Company's applications or by email at info@sessia.com and describe the violation in detail. Upon receipt of a report by the Company, all parties will be given the opportunity to provide evidence, confirmations, and written statements.

8.3.

If it is confirmed that the Partner has violated the rules of this Code, the Company reserves the right to suspend the Partner’s activities for some time (at the Company’s discretion). During this time, the Partner loses the rights to all operating privileges without exception and does not have the right to sign new Partners. The partner is deprived of the right to purchase and receive rewards and gifts and is denied access to the Company’s applications. Within 10 days from this moment, the Partner must contact the Company to clarify the situation.

8.4.

In the event of a gross and systematic violation of this Code by the Partner, the Company has the right to unilaterally terminate the Agreement with the Partner without any compensation.
